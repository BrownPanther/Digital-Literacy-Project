## UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. Unlike traditional scams, UPI fraud often does not require the victim to share their PIN directly, but instead manipulates them into approving payment requests.

A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a phone call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer. The scammer claims that money needs to be “received” and sends a UPI collect request. The victim is instructed to enter their UPI PIN to accept the request. In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a payment from the victim’s account to the scammer.

In some cases, fraudsters also send fake links or QR codes, which, when scanned, initiate a payment instead of receiving money. College students and first-time digital payment users are frequent targets due to their limited awareness of such scams.

The consequences of UPI fraud include immediate financial loss, compromised bank accounts, and emotional stress. Recovery of money is often difficult if the fraud is not reported quickly.

This case highlights the importance of understanding how digital payment systems work and being cautious while handling unknown payment requests or links.
